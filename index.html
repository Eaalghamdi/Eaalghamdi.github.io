I am an assistant professor of computational linguistics at King Abdulaziz University. I am the director of the AILLA Lab . My research interests lie at the intersection of Applied Linguistics, Human-Computer Interactions (HCI), and Machine Learning.

In my PhD work, I investigated what makes a video difficult for language learners and developed computational models to automatically assess difficulty in videos for use in language assessment, research, and instruction. I also built the Automated Video Analysis (AUVANA) , a cross-platform desktop application for extracting, computing, and visualizing complexity in videos. I was supervised by Dr. Paul Gruba and Dr. Edaurdo Velloso.

I am interested in building innovative tools to help language learners, teachers, researchers, and educators. Before starting my PhD, I was the founding director of KAUx, a MOOC platform at King Abdulaziz University.

I am excited to launch my lab, the AI in Language Learning & Assessment Lab (https://t.co/azNrVjihZQ). I collaborate with scientists from Academia (Oxford, Cambridge, Monash, Melbourne) and industry (Adobe & Google) on research related to multimodality, readability, & assessment.
