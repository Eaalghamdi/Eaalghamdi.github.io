I am an incoming assistant professor of computational linguistics at King Abdulaziz University. I am currently investigating video difficulty and complexity. My research interests lie at the intersection of Applied Linguistics, Human-Computer Interactions (HCI), and Machine Learning.

In my PhD work, I investigated what makes a video difficult for language learners and developed computational models to automatically assess difficulty in videos for use in language assessment, research, and instruction. I also built the Automated Video Analysis (AUVANA) , a cross-platform desktop application for extracting, computing, and visualizing complexity in videos. I was supervised by Dr. Paul Gruba and Dr. Edaurdo Velloso.

I am interested in building innovative tools (websites, plugins, mobile apps, desktop apps, etc.) to help language learners, teachers, researchers, and educators. Before starting my PhD, I was the founding director of KAUx, a MOOC platform at King Abdulaziz University.
