Hello! My name is Emad A. Alghamdi. I am a second-year PhD student in the Faculty of Languages and Linguistics at the University of Melbourne. I am also a Research Fellow in the Digital Studio at the same university. In my PhD work, I am trying to answer one research question: what makes a video complex for language learners? To answer this question, I draw on various fields of studies (e.g., Linguistics, Discourse and Text Comprehension, Computer Vision, and Phonetics) to build an understanding and then develop a Machine Learning classifier that could help in classifying videotexts based on varying degrees of complexity. I am also working on developing a video playing interface for language learners.

Before starting my PhD, I was the founder and director of KAUx, a MOOC platform at King Abdulaziz University. I received an MA in English (TESOL) and MS in Educational Technology, from Missouri State University, USA.
